Robert Poppit (1774-1838) was baptized in Hissington1 and lived his whole life in and around Hyssington, where he was a coal miner.2 

From PSkellon: Robert’s line can be traced back to a Robert Popitt3 who was born about 1670 in the Minsterley valley, which runs SW from Shrewsbury, Shropshire4 (where Robert Popitt was born) to the Welsh border in Montgomeryshire. This valley has been used for lead mining since Roman times.5 The Poppett men were miners until the late 19th century. Poppitts were churchwardens at St Ethelreadas church in Hyssington6 and worked the nearby barytes (barium) mines on Corndon Hill7, known as White Grit, since its use as white pigment in paint prior to titanium dioxide.

source: BBC website

Around 1800 the branch including our shared ancestor, Robert Popitt’s father Joseph Poppit, moved to the East Shropshire coalfield around Donnington & Lilleshall10. Joseph was a Charter Master (gang boss) responsible to the owner for running the mine. His gravestone and several other family ones are still visible leaning against the church wall in Lilleshall.1112

RLP’s mother Elizabeth Llewellyn died in 1836 and his father Robert Poppit died in 183813.
